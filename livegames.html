Live Game


Live Game Guide

1. Create a group party.

One person creates a PSN party chat and adds all those involved.

- 2 players + main commentator + co-commentator(if there is one)


2. Sharing your voice online.

All party members need to check/set settings inside party chat to have your voice shared online.


3. Shareplay and Broadcast.

Shareplay:

One player creates a share play (done inside the party chat) the main commentator will join. The main commentator can then watch game live.

Broadcast:

The other player broadcasts the game on yoututbe or twitch and shares link to discord for viewers. When creating broadcast make sure to tick the box that allows audio from party chat to stream to the broadcast so people can hear the commentators.


4. Changing game audio settings.

At the kick off screen before match starts you can change audio settings in the settings tab.

To create a better experience for viewers the person doing broadcast should:

- Turn the in game commentary to 0
- Turn the stadium/crowd fx to 2 or 3.

Also to help main commentator concentrate easier, shareplay player should:

- turn commentary to 0.
- turn crowd/stadium fx to 5.


5. Audio options for players during game?

To make sure your voice (Screams/cheers) are not shared to the broadcast MUTE your microphone/headest.

Listen to commentators

If you want to listen to the commentators you need not do anything, you can choose to have the party chat audio come through TV and listen on TV or you can choose it to come trough headset and listen on headset.

Dont want listen commentators

In this case what to do is make sure party chat is only coming through the headset and game audio through TV.
Keep headset plugged into the controller but take off your ears (and turn volume to 0). Play game and listen to audio through TV, it will be low volume sound fx only (no konami commentary)
